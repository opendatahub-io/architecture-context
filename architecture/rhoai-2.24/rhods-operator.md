## Purpose
**Short**: The rhods-operator is the central platform operator for Red Hat OpenShift AI (RHOAI), managing the lifecycle of all AI/ML platform components on OpenShift.

**Detailed**: The rhods-operator (also known as the opendatahub-operator) is a Go-based Kubernetes operator built on controller-runtime that orchestrates the deployment, configuration, and lifecycle management of the Red Hat OpenShift AI platform. It uses two primary custom resources: `DSCInitialization` (DSCI) for platform-level initialization (namespace creation, monitoring, service mesh, trusted CA bundles, authentication/authorization) and `DataScienceCluster` (DSC) for managing individual AI/ML component enablement.

The operator follows a layered architecture where the DSC controller creates singleton component CRs (e.g., Dashboard, Kserve, Workbenches) based on user configuration, and each component has its own dedicated controller that watches its CR and deploys the actual component manifests from kustomize overlays stored in `/opt/manifests/`. The operator supports three platform modes: ManagedRhoai (addon-based managed service), SelfManagedRhoai (customer-managed RHOAI), and OpenDataHub (upstream community edition).

The operator also manages platform services including authentication (admin/allowed group RBAC), monitoring (Prometheus, AlertManager, OpenTelemetry, Tempo), trusted CA bundle distribution, secret generation with OAuthClient management, and optional Istio Service Mesh integration for KServe model serving. It provides admission webhooks for validating and mutating DSC, DSCI, InferenceService, Notebook, and Kueue-related resources.
